Clustering on Time Series Data for Lipid Profiles of Celiac Disease Progression

  • Synopsis: The focus of this project was to develop a new method for analyzing time series data. The data set we chose focused on lipid profiles for patients with celiac disease. Utilizing Python's Euclidean distance metric and the clustering algorithm KMedoids(), we were able to cluster the data and then analyze trends in those cluster's centers for each lipid, rather than analyzing lipid progressions separately.
  • Contributors: BJ Stubbs, Professor Soha Hassoun

Viz-a-Thon 2021 Entry

  • Synopsis: The goal of the 2021 Viz-a-Thon competition was to create a database and visualization tool to help people understand the growing obesity crisis in the United States. Using R, my team and I created an RShiny app that allows users to view data pertaining to several key factors in determining obesity risk, including socieconomic status and family history.

STEM Capstone

  • Synopsis: For my high school capstone project, I developed predictive models using R and a Poisson Distribution for my school's admissions office. I took a look at the number of students admitted each year, their demographics, and the percentage of each group that chose to attend our institution. I was able to present my findings to the admission committee in an effort to promote more diverse admissions to the school.

Differentiating Between HIV Positive and HIV Negative Patients Based on Immune Responses

  • Synopsis: As part of my summer internship at Johns Hopkins Department of Applied Mathematics and Statistics, I was tasked with determining the greatest variations in immune responses between HIV positive and negative patients given a dataset that was collected through the medical center.
